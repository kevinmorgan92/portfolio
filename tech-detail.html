Computer Technology

The module aimed to cultivate an appreciation for the evolution of computer systems, starting from early prototypes to modern-day advancements. It focused on developing an understanding of the fundamental architecture of computer systems, including the functions and operations of their components, as well as their interrelationship with the BIOS and operating system within a personal computer. Additionally, the module explored the operation of various input/output devices connected to computers, enhancing students' knowledge of data input and output processes. Lastly, it aimed to foster an appreciation for the representation of data and text within a computer, covering topics related to data encoding, storage, and retrieval. Through these objectives, the module equipped students with a comprehensive understanding of computer systems and their essential components, preparing them for further exploration and specialization in the field.

The module aimed to foster recognition of the technologies that facilitated the evolution of computer systems from their early prototypes to the sophisticated modern computers of today. It emphasized the appreciation of the complexity inherent in modern computer systems. Additionally, students gained an understanding of how information is represented, stored, and processed within a computer system. They were able to construct basic digital logic circuits that allowed computer systems to interact with the external world. Furthermore, the module covered the tools and techniques used in evaluating computer networks, providing students with a comprehensive understanding of networking concepts. Through these objectives, students developed the knowledge and skills necessary to comprehend the evolution, intricacies, and practical applications of computer systems in contemporary contexts.

The core topics of the module are as follows.

  • Computing Fundamentals, Numeracy and History - Introduction to fundamental concepts of computing, including numerical calculations, and exploration of the historical development of computers
  • Logical Circuits, Fetch-Decode Execute, Processor History - Study of logical circuits and their role in computer systems, understanding the fetch-decode-execute cycle, and exploring the history of processors
  • Buses, Motherboards and Floating Point Notation - Examination of computer buses and their function, understanding motherboard components and their interconnections, and introduction to floating-point notation for representing decimal numbers in binary form
  • Storage and Compression - Exploration of various storage devices and their characteristics, along with compression techniques for reducing the size of digital data
  • I/O Devices - Study of input/output devices used for interacting with computer systems, such as keyboards, mice, displays, and printers
  • Network Fundamentals - Introduction to the basics of computer networking, including concepts like IP addressing, subnetting, routing, and network protocols
  • Version Management with Git - Hands-on experience with Git, a popular version control system, for managing and tracking changes in software development projects
  • Assembly Programming - Introduction to assembly language programming, understanding the low-level instructions and how they are executed by the computer's processor
  • Face Detection and Recognition - Exploring the concepts and algorithms behind face detection and recognition techniques, including the use of machine learning and computer vision
  • TCP/IP Networking - In-depth understanding of the TCP/IP protocol suite, including the layers of the protocol stack and their functions in facilitating network communication

The module comprised of two class tests making up 40 and 60% respectivley. The first test was focused on Lab work throughout the module, Git management, Assembley programming, Face and image recognition. The second exam was based on the Lecture and Tutorial material, Computing and Networking fundamentals. For this module i gained a combined grade of 85%
