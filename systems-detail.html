Systems Software

The studied module aimed to provide students with a comprehensive understanding of the concepts, structure, and mechanisms of systems software. It focused on enabling students to apply these concepts to analyze, design, implement, and evaluate modern-day solutions. The module reinforced design principles through practical examples from real-world scenarios, allowing students to grasp the practical application of their knowledge. Additionally, the module emphasized the importance of considering legal, privacy, and ethical constraints or opportunities within a company, fostering an appreciation for the impact of these factors on software development

Through this module, students gained a solid understanding of the concepts underlying modern systems software design, including architectural patterns, software modeling, scalability, security, and performance optimization. They developed the skills to analyze, design, implement, and evaluate contemporary solutions. The module also emphasized the evaluation of professional, legal, moral, and ethical issues surrounding technology exploitation, ensuring students were aware of their responsibilities and the broader implications of their work. Additionally, students engaged in research to explore cutting-edge breakthroughs in systems software design and their practical applications in business contexts. Overall, this module provided students with a comprehensive knowledge base, ethical awareness, and research skills in the field of modern systems software design.

The core topics of the module are as follows.

  • Computer Systems Overview - Introduction to computer system components and interaction
  • OS Overview - Overview of operating systems and their functions
  • Processes - Creation, scheduling, and management of processes
  • Threads - Lightweight units of execution within a process
  • Concurrency - Design and implementation of concurrent systems
  • File permissions - Setting and modifying file permissions
  • Shells - Interacting with the operating system using command-line interfaces
  • Ubuntu Unix commands - Usage of Unix commands in the Ubuntu operating system
  • Bash scripts - Writing scripts to automate tasks using Bash
  • Security in UNIX - User permissions, access controls, and security practices

The module consisted of two class tests worth 50% each. Covering all aspects of the module being taught. I achieved an overall grade of 85% for this module.
